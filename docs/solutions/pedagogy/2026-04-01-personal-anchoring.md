## Context

In training presentations, theory is more impactful when participants have a personal reference point.

## Problem

Presenting theory directly ("VibeCoding has 4 levels") creates passive absorption. Participants don't connect the framework to their own experience.

## Solution

Before introducing a theoretical framework, ask participants to self-position:

1. **Ask a personal question** related to the upcoming theory (billboard question slide)
2. **Visualize the spectrum** with a gradient bar showing the range of possible answers
3. **Acknowledge their position** ("Your answer today will change")
4. **Then introduce theory** — participants naturally map themselves onto the framework

### Example sequence

- "How much of your code is AI-generated?" → spectrum 0%↔100%
- "Do you review AI-generated code?" → spectrum Never↔Always
- Then: "VibeCoding level 1 = never review, level 4 = review at boundaries"

Each participant mentally recalls their answer and evaluates the gap.

### Other applications

- "How much time do you spend on testing?" → before TDD principles
- "Do you write requirements before coding?" → before requirements engineering module
- "How often do you refactor?" → before clean code session

## Applicability

Any training module introducing a framework with levels, scales, or positions. Effective for audiences of 5-20 participants where self-reflection is possible.
